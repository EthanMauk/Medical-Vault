**ai**: - **Term**: Mechanism of Injury  
  **Definition**: The specific manner in which a physical injury occurred, describing how, with what force, and to which part of the body force was applied.  
  **Example**: A penetrating trauma from a gunshot wound to the abdomen [^1].  

- **Term**: Nature of Illness  
  **Definition**: The underlying medical condition causing a patient’s signs and symptoms.  
  **Example**: Shortness of breath due to asthma [^2].  

- **Term**: Therapeutic Communication  
  **Definition**: A set of communication techniques designed to encourage patients to express their feelings and foster a positive relationship with the provider.  
  **Key Techniques**: Facilitation, Reflection, Empathy, Clarification, Confrontation, Explanation, Summary [^3].  

- **Term**: Medical Necessity  
  **Definition**: The condition where it would be unsafe or impossible to transport a patient by any other means.  
  **Examples**: Patient unconscious, in shock, exhibiting stroke signs, or requiring oxygen during transport [^4].  

- **Term**: Scene Size-Up  
  **Definition**: The initial assessment performed upon arrival at the scene to ensure personal and patient safety.  
  **Key Steps**: BSI/PPE, identify environmental hazards (fire, traffic, weapons), assess number of patients, determine mechanism of injury, and evaluate need for specialized equipment [^5].  

- **Term**: Patient Care Report (PCR)  
  **Definition**: A document used to record all aspects of patient care, ensuring continuity of care and compliance with legal standards.  
  **Key Components**: Chief Complaint, Mechanism of Injury/Nature of Illness, Level of Consciousness, Vitals, Initial Assessment, Transport Information [^6].  

- **Term**: Consent  
  **Definition**: A patient’s permission for treatment; must be given by an adult who is conscious and rational.  
  **Types**: Expressed (verbal or non-verbal), Implied (for unconscious patients in life-threatening situations), Involuntary (when a legal guardian cannot be reached) [^7].  

- **Term**: Prefixes, Roots, Suffixes in Medical Terminology  
  **Definition**: Medical terms are built from roots (core meaning), prefixes (modifiers at the beginning), suffixes (specifiers at the end), and combining vowels.  
  **Example**: *Nephropathy* = nephr/o (kidney) + pathy (disease) [^8].  

- **Term**: Positional and Directional Terms  
  **Definition**: Used to describe body parts in anatomical position; e.g., anterior/posterior, superior/inferior, proximal/distal.  
  **Example**: "Abduction" is movement away from the midline; "flexion" decreases joint angle [^9].  

- **Term**: Incident Command System (ICS)  
  **Definition**: A standardized framework for organizing emergency responses across agencies.  
  **Key Principles**: Unified Command, Chain of Command, Unity of Command, Modularity, Span of Control (ideally 3–7 personnel per supervisor) [^10].
