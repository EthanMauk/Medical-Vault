### **Section A: Multiple Choice (2 points each)**

1. Which of the following best defines *Body Substance Isolation (BSI)*?  
   a) The use of personal protective equipment to prevent exposure during patient contact  
   b) A legal document allowing patients to refuse treatment despite medical advice  
   c) A system for managing emergency scenes with unified command and chain of authority  
   d) A method used to assess mental status using the AVPU scale  

2. What is the primary goal of a Scene Size-Up?  
   a) To determine patient age and developmental stage  
   b) To ensure all patients are transported immediately  
   c) To protect responders, partners, and patients from harm  
   d) To initiate triage procedures before any care begins  

3. Which of these is an example of *expressed consent*?  
   a) A patient signs a form stating they agree to treatment  
   b) A provider assumes the patient agrees based on silence  
   c) A child nods their head in agreement during a conversation  
   d) A guardian gives verbal permission after discussion  

4. In which chapter would you find information about *standard of care* and its relationship to negligence?  
   a) Chapter 5: Medical Terminology  
   b) Chapter 3: Medical, Legal, Ethical Issues  
   c) Chapter 4: Communication and Documentation  
   d) Chapter 6: Human Body  

5. What does the AVPU scale evaluate in assessing level of consciousness?  
   a) Blood pressure and heart rate  
   b) Response to verbal and painful stimuli  
   c) Orientation to person, place, time, and event  
   d) Ability to walk or stand independently

### **Section B: True/False (1 point each)**

6. A patient who refuses treatment has the right to refuse care if they are conscious and have decision-making capacity. [^1]  
7. The *Right to Refuse* applies only to children under 18 years old. [^2]  
8. Closed-Loop Communication ensures that instructions are received correctly by repeating them back in exact language. [^3]  
9. Infection control practices include wearing PPE and following Standard Precautions, which are part of BSI. [^4]  
10. The *Standard of Care* is determined solely by state laws and does not consider accepted medical guidelines. [^5]

### **Section C: Short Answer (3 points each)**

11. Explain the difference between *anatomy* and *physiology*. Provide one example for each.  

12. Describe how a provider can demonstrate *informed consent* when treating a patient. Include at least three elements that must be communicated.  

13. List four environmental hazards identified during a Scene Size-Up and explain why they are critical to identify.  

14. Define *abandonment* in the context of emergency medical services, and contrast it with *against medical advice*.  

15. Why is *cumulative stress* particularly relevant for EMS personnel? Provide two symptoms and one consequence of prolonged exposure.
