**Compassion Fatigue** is a common secondary stress disorder experienced by health care providers and first responders. It is characterized by a lessening compassion over time, Unlike [[Posttraumatic Stress Disorder|PTSD]] which is caused by direct exposure to traumatic incidents, compassion fatigue is a reaction to caring for others who have experienced trauma. 

### Symptoms
- High absenteeism
- Difficult relationships with colleagues and coworkers
- Inability to work in teams
- Aggressive behavior towards patients
- Strong negative attitudes towards work
- Lack of empathy for patients 
- Increased Stress

Compassion Fatigue is a result of [[Cumulative Stress|cumulative stress]], and its [[Signs|signs]] and [[Symptoms|symptoms]] may not be obvious.
