**Wellness** is the active pursuit of a state of good health. Maintaining wellness takes conscious effort and work similar to maintaining physical fitness or knowledge. For EMS providers, wellness is critical to **safety, performance, and long-term health**.

A key aspect of wellness is **stress management**, as first responders are frequently exposed to high-stress, high-stakes situations.

Strategies for stress management:
- Minimize stressors
- Change work hours/partner
- Talk about your feelings
- Seek counseling
- Do not obsess over situations
- Expand your social support system

Research shows that first responders are at a higher risk of death by suicide than from line-of-duty injuries. After unintentional injury, suicide is the leading cause of death among EMS, fire, and law enforcement personnel. Maintaining **wellness is not optional** — it is essential for provider safety, patient care, and long-term career sustainability.
