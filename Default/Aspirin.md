# Aspirin
**Aspirin** (acetylsalicylic acid) is a common anti-inflammatory medication used to reduce fever or pain. 

>[!indicators]+ Indications #card
> - Mild to moderate fever
> - Acute [[Angina]] 
> - [[Myocardial Infarction]]
> - Suspected [[Acute Coronary Syndrome]] (ACS)

>[!warning]+ Contraindications #card 
> - Unconscious or unable to chew and swallow
> - Actively bleeding or bleeding disorder
> - Suspected [[stroke]]
## Administration
Aspirin is typically administered [[orally]] through chewable tablets at 160-325 mg for an adult.

>[!field]+ Aspirin versus Nitroglycerin #card
> Aspirin takes **priority** over [[Nitroglycerin]] when both are indicated. Aspirin reduces mortality in [[Acute Coronary Syndrome]] and [[Myocardial Infarction]], while nitroglycerin primarily alleviates symptoms

## Mechanism #card
Aspirin works by inhibiting cyclooxygenase [[enzymes]], preventing the production of [[prostaglandins]] and [[thromboxanes]] which are key components of the inflammatory, pain and clotting response 

## Considerations #card
- **Severe bleeding risk** if given to patients on [[anti-coagulants]]
- [[Reyes Syndrome]] in peds with fever
