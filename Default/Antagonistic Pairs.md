**Antagonistic Pairs** are pairs of muscles that work in opposite directions to create movements, when one contracts the other relaxes. 

**Examples**:
- Biceps and Triceps
- Hamstrings and Quadriceps

**Agonist**: The muscle that contracts or shortens to create the movement
**Antagonist**: The muscle that relaxes and elongates to allow movement
**Reversibility**: When reversing the action, the muscles switch roles
