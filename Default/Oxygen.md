# Oxygen  
**Oxygen**

>[!indicators]+ Indications #card
> - [[Hypoxia]] (SpO₂ < 94%)
> - Respiratory distress (e.g., [[asthma]], [[COPD]] exacerbation)
> - Suspected [[myocardial ischemia]]
> - [[Cardiac Arrest]]
> - [[Anaphylaxis]]
> - Suspected [[Pneumothorax]]

>[!warning]+ Contraindications #card
>- None in emergencies  
> - Use caution in certain COPD patients to avoid hypercapnia

## Administration
Titrate to maintain [[SpO₂]] 94–98% (88–92% in COPD patients). For mild to moderate respiratory distress, or those who cannot tolerate a full mask, nasal cannula or blow-by-oxygen is recommended. 

>[!study]+ Oxygen Routes, Flow and Concentration #card 
>- via [[nasal cannula]] (1–6 L/min) (24-44%)
>- via Simple mask (8-10L/min) (35-60%)
>- via [[non-rebreather]] mask (10–15 L/min)  (60-90%)
>- via [[Blow-by-Oxygen]] (15L/min) (25-50%)
>- via [[Bag Valve Mask|BVM]] with reservoir (15L/min) (<100%)



## Mechanism #card
Oxygen increases the amount of oxygen available in the [[alveoli]], allowing more oxygen to [[Diffusion|diffuse]] into the blood and reach tissues, supporting cellular [[metabolism]].

## Considerations #card

>[!field] Cylinder Etiquette 
> - Avoid leavings cylinders upright, a falling cylinder can become a missle. 
> - Always check [[Pin Index]] and verify with oxygen regulator
> - Medical grade oxygen is labeled “Oxygen U.S.P.”

## Adverse Effects 

>[!transparent] Adverse Effects #card
> - Oxidative stress
> - Delayed recognition of patient deterioration
> - Rebound hypoxia from oxygen withdrawal
> - Constriction of blood vessels reducing blood flow
