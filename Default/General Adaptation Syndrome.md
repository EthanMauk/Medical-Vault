**General Adaptation Syndrome (GAS)** is a model developed by Hans Selye to describe how the body responds to prolonged stress. It outlines a predictable three-stage physiological pattern that occurs regardless of the type of stressor (physical, psychological, environmental, etc.).

The three stages are:
- Alarm reaction
	- Release of epinephrine and norepinephrine
	- Increased [[Heart Rate|HR]] and [[Blood Pressure|BP]]
	- Dilated pupils and bronchioles
	- Release of cortisol
- Resistance
	- Body appears stable under physiological cost
- Exhaustion
	- Cortisol dysregulation 
	- Immune suppression
	- Fatigue
	- Adrenal glands lose secretions 
	- Organs degenerate
