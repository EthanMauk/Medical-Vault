## Overview
A **Bag Valve Mask (BVM)**, is a [[Manual Ventilator Device]] used to provide [[Positive Pressure|positive-pressure]] [[Ventilation|ventilation]] to a patient who is not breathing adequately (slow, shallow respirations or low [[Tidal Volume|tidal volume]]).

## Anatomy
A typical **BVM** is composed of:
 - Transparent Face Mask
 - [[Non-rebreathing]] outlet valve
 - Oxygen reservoir bag
 - One-way inlet valve supporting 15L/min
![[BVM_AmbuBag_WebImage.jpg]]



It allows providers to **manually move air into the lungs** by squeezing a self-inflating bag connected to a mask or airway device.

Providers may **assist ventilations by squeezing the bag in sync with the patient’s natural inhalation**, augmenting the breath and increasing tidal volume.

Unless contraindicated, an airway adjunct ([[Nasopharyngeal Airway|NPA]]/[[Oropharyngeal Ariway|OPA]]) should be used with BVM. 

A BVM may also be used to **oxygenate/saturate a patient prior to [[Intubation|intubation]]** (pre-oxygenation) and during procedures such as [[Resuscitation|resuscitation]]
