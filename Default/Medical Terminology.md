## Anatomy of Terminology
Medical terms are composed of distinct parts:
- [[Roots Tables|Word Root]] - Foundational Meaning of Word
- [[Prefixes Table|Prefix]] - Modifiers at the Beginning of Word
- [[Suffixes Table|Suffix]] - Specifiers at the End of Word
- Combining Vowel - Combines non prefix terms for cadence

Every term has a word root, and may have none, one, or many prefixes and suffixes.   

### Examples
***Nephropathy***: nephr/o/pathy
nephr/o: kidney
pathy: disease

***Dysuria***: dys/ur/ia
dys: difficulty
ur: urine
ia: condition of 
### Combining Form
The most common combining vowel is "o", but it can vary depending on the root word. For convenience the associated combining vowel is often displayed with the root word in a combining form. 

***cardi/o*** (heart)
***gastr/o*** (stomach)
***hepat/o*** (liver)
***arthr/o*** (joint)

### Plural Rules
Words with certain endings are modified past adding an s when going from a singular to plural form. 

| Singular | Plural | Example             |
| -------- | ------ | ------------------- |
| a        | ae     | vertebra/vertebrae  |
| is       | es     | diagnosis/diagnoses |
| ex       | ices   | apex/apices         |
| on/um    | a      | ovum/ova            |
| us       | i      | bronchus/bronchi    |

### Direction, Movement and Position

| Direction          | Meaning                                     |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| Anterior/Posterior | Front Surface/Back Surface                  |
| Right/Left         | Patients Right/Left                         |
| Superior/Inferior  | Close to Head/Feet                          |
| Proximal/Distal    | Close/Far To/From Attachement               |
| Medial/Lateral     | Close/Far To/From Midline                   |
| Deep/Superficial   | Farthest/Closest to the surface on the skin |
| Bilateral          | On both sides                               |
| Palmar             | Palm of Hand                                |
| Plantar            | Sole of Feet                                |

| Movement  | Meaning                   |
| --------- | ------------------------- |
| Flexion   | Decreasing Angle of Joint |
| Extension | Increasing Angle of Joint |
| Adduction | Motion Towards Midline    |
| Abduction | Motion Away From Midline  |
