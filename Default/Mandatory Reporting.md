**Mandatory reporting** laws require EMS providers (and other designated professionals) to report certain suspected abuse to authorities, even without patient consent.

It commonly includes:
- **Suspected child abuse**
- **Elder abuse or dependent adult abuse**
- **Injuries from violent crimes** (e.g., gunshot or stab wounds)
- Certain **drug-related injuries** (varies by state)

Key points:
- You only need **reasonable suspicion**, not proof.
- Reports must be made in **good faith**.
- Good faith reporters are typically granted **legal immunity**.
- It **overrides patient confidentiality**.
- Failure to report can result in criminal penalties or loss of certification.
