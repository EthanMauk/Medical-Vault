**Scope of Practice** refers to the care a provider is legally authorized to perform for a patient.

An EMS provider’s scope is determined by:
- **National standards** (such as the National EMS Scope of Practice Model)
- **State laws and regulations**, which may restrict or expand national standards
- **Local medical director protocols**, which further define what is allowed in a specific system

A medical director may expand a provider’s scope in real time by giving **direct online medical control orders**, allowing a provider to perform an intervention that is not normally included in standing protocols — provided it is legally permissible within state regulations.
