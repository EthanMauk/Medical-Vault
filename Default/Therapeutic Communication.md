**Therapeutic Communication** is a set of communication techniques and strategies to encourage patients to express how they are feeling and foster a positive relationship with the patient. 

#### Factors to Consider
Age, Eye Contact, Body language, Sex, Clothing, Posture, Tempo, Volume, Environment

#### Golden Rules of Therapeutic Rapport
- Make and keep eye contact
- Provide your name and use the patients proper name
- Tell the patient the truth 
- Use language the patient can understand
- Be mindful of what you say about the patient to others
- Be mindful of your body language, avoid frustrated or threatening postering
- Speak slowly, clearly, and distinctly
- Allow time for the patient to respond
- Act and speak in a calm, confident manner
- Face the person and try to be on eye level

#### Therapeutic Communication Techniques
| Technique     | Definition                                                                       | Example                                                                                            |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Facilitation  | Encourage the patient to talk more                                               | "Could you tell me more about that?"                                                               |
| Pause         | Do not speak                                                                     | Giving the patient space and time to think                                                         |
| Reflection    | Restating a patients statement to confirm understanding                          | Patient: "I am so depressed".  EMT: "I understand you are feeling depressed"                       |
| Empathy       | Be sensitive to the patients feelings and thoughts                               | Use eye contact and, if appropriate, touching to reinforce communication,                          |
| Clarification | Ask the patient to explain what they meant                                       | EMT: "Can you tell me how you are feeling sick, what feels wrong"                                  |
| Confrontation | Make the patient who is in denial or shock focus on the matter at hand           | We will talk about your medicines in a moment, tell me about your chest pain                       |
| Explanation   | Provide factual information to support a conversation                            | "We have checked your blood sugar and pressure and dont see anything we need to immediately treat" |
| Summary       | Provide the patient with an overview of the conversation and steps you will take | "We will be taking you to the ED to care for your chest pain"                                      |
#### Techniques to Avoid
- Providing false hope
- Giving unsolicited advice
- Asking leading questions
- Talking too much
- Interrupting
- Asking why
- Using authoritative language
- Speaking in jargon
