# Oral Glucose
**Oral Glucose**

>[!indicators]+ Indications #card
> - Altered Mental Status
> - Blood Glucose < 60 mg/dL

>[!warning]+ Contraindications #card
> - Unable to swallow 
> - Blood Glucose > 60 mg/dL


## Administration #card
EMT’s are only allowed to administer glucose orally, at a dose of ½ to 1 tube. 

## Mechanism #card
Oral glucose works so quickly because it’s made of **simple sugars (monosaccharides)** like **glucose** itself, rather than complex carbohydrates (polysaccharides) found in most foods. Simple sugars don’t need digestion—they are rapidly absorbed directly into the bloodstream from the gastrointestinal tract, producing a fast rise in blood glucose levels.

## Adverse effects 
Adverse Effects to Oral Glucose are significantly more likely if taken on an empty stomach.

>[!transparent|cols] Adverse Effects #card
>>- Diarrhea
>>- Vomitting
>>- Tremors
>
>>- Headaches
>>- Dizziness
>>- Nausea
