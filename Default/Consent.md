**Consent** is a persons permission for treatment. It must be made by an adult who is conscious, rational, and [[Informed Consent|informed]]. A patient who meets this requirement has the right to refuse care ([[Against Medical Advice]]). Going against a patients consent can be grounds for criminal and civil action. 

### Types of Consent
**[[Expressed consent]]** is the type of consent given when the patient explicitly acknowledges you that they want to receive care. It may be verbal or nonverbal, nodding yes qualifies for expressed consent. 

**[[Implied consent]]** is when a person is unconscious or unable of making an informed rational decision about care and/or are unable to give consent. The law assumes the patient would consent to care and transport. Implied consend is only applicable when there is a threat to life or limb.

**[[Involuntary Consent]]** is applicable when an adult patient is incapable of making a rational decision and their legal guardian or conservator cannot be reached.
