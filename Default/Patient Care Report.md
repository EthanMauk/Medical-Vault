The **Patient Care Report**, also called the prehospital care report, is the document used to record all aspect of care your patient received, There are written and electronic versions (PCR and ePCR). The primary role of a PCR is to ensure efficient continuity of care. 

**Functions of a PCR**
- Transfer of information and [[Continuity of Care]]
- Compliance and legal documentation
- Administrative information
- Reimbursement
- Education
- Data Collection for research and [[CQI]]

**Information in a PCR**
- [[Chief Complaint]]
- [[Mechanism of Injury]] / [[Nature of Illness]]
- [[Level of Consciousness]] 
- [[Vitals]]
- Initial Assessment
- Patient Demographics
- Transport information

**Essential Times**
- Time incident was reported
- Time EMS was notified 
- Time EMS arrived on scene
- Time EMS makes contact with patient
- Time EMS unit left scene
- Time EMS arrived at the receiving facility
- Time patient was transferred care
- Time unit was back in service

The narrative is an essential part of a PCR that describes the events of the EMS call. There are two common methods of standardizing the narrative format.

**CHART**:
- Chief Complaint
- History
- Assessments
- Treatments
- Transport

**SOAP**:
- Subjective
- Objective
- Assessment
- Plan

Documenting medical necessity is important in the PCR because Medicare and Medicaid will only reimburse ambulance transport if the services are documented as being [[Medical Necessity]].
