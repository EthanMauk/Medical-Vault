# Anatomy of Lower Airway
The **lower airway** is composed of branching tubes which get more numerous and narrow as they progress through the lungs. 

The **trachea** divides into right and left **bronchi**, which divides into lobar bronchi, then segmental bronchi, terminal **bronchioles** which is the smallest airways without **alveoli**.

All these bronchi make up the *Conducting Airways*, which are regions of [[Dead Space]] that lead inspired air to gas exchange regions. 

The terminal bronchioles divide into respiratory bronchioles which have some alveoli, and finally split into alveolar ducts, which are lines fully with alveoli. These alveolated regions are known as the *Respiratory Zone*.

### Gas Exchange

| Inhaled Air:<br>- ~78% Nitrogen (N₂)<br>- ~21% Oxygen (O₂)<br>- >1% Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) | **Exhaled Air**:<br>- ~79% Nitrogen (N₂)<br>- ~16% Oxygen (O₂)<br>- 3-5% Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

Breathing is controlled by the brain. When the [[Medulla]] detects a decrease in [[pH]] in the [[Cerebrospinal Fluid]] (caused by buildup of CO<sub>2</sub>), it stimulates the [[Phrenic Nerve]], sending a signal to the [[Diaphragm]] to contract faster. As the diaphragm becomes more active the [[Respiratory Rate]] and [[Tidal Volume]] increase. 

As a fail-safe the body as have a system to kickstart respiration when oxygen levels fall too far called [[Hypoxic Drive]].
