**Joints** are the structures where between two or more bones. A typical Synovial Joint consists of: bone ends, a fibrous joint capsule, synovial membranes, and ligaments. 

### Nomenclature 
Most joints are named after their adjoining bones
- The **sternoclavicular joint** connects the sternum and clavicle
- The **acromioclavicular joint** connects the acromion and clavicle
- The **tibiofemoral joint** connects the tibia and femur

## Anatomy
#### [[Ligaments]] 
Strong bands of fibrous connective tissue that 
- Connect bone to bone
- Reinforce and stabilize joints

#### [[Cartilage]]
Covers the ends of bones which 
- Reduce Friction 
- Absorb Shock

#### [[Joint Capsule]]
A tough fibrous sac that 
-  Encloses the joint
- Holds articulating bones together
- contains[[ Synovial Membrane]]

### Synovial Joints
Joints have different levels of [[Range of Motion]] and [[Degrees of Freedom]]. This is determined by the extent to which the ligaments hold the bone ends together and by the configuration of the bones. 

- The **Shoulder Joint** is a **ball-and-socket joint**, which allows rotation and bending
- The **Finger Joints**, **elbow, and knees** are **hinge joints**, with its motion restriction to [[Flextion]] and [[Extension]].

### Other Joints
- Joints will little range of motion are called [[symphyses]].
- There are fibrous joints, which have no joint cavity and have little to no movement such as the skull sutures
