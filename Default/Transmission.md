**Transmission** is the way infectious diseases spread.

**Contact Transmission** is the movement of an organism from one person to another through physical touch. 

Contact transmission can be direct or indirect. Direct Contact transmission occurs when an organism is moved directly from one person to another. 

Indirect contact transmission occurs when infectious agents are transferred to a contaminated intermediary surface, such as door knobs, beds, or tables or needlesticks. 
### Types of Transmission 
**Food-borne Transmission** is when pathogens contaminate food or water, such as *E. Coli* or *Listeria*, and spreads to other individuals

**Vector-borne Transmission** is the spread of infection by animals. Fleas, mosquitos and bats are common carriers for vector-borne diseases, such as *Lyme disease* and *the Black Death.* 

**Airborne Transmission** involves the spread of an infectious agent through droplets or dust. The common cold is an airborne disease. Sneezing can transmit diseases through direct contact and airborne. 

**Vertical Transmission** involves direct transfer of infection between mother and child, through the placenta or breastmilk. Toxoplasmosis, Rubella and HIV can be transmitted vertically.  

[[Standard Precautions]] exist to help prevent transmission of infectious diseases.
