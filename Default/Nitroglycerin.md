# Overview
**Nitroglycerin** is a common [[Vasodilator]] used to provide relief from chest pain. 

  >[!indicators]+ Nitro Indications #card
 >- Acute [[Angina]] (chest pain due to [[Myocardial Ischemia]])
 >- Suspected [[Acute Coronary Syndrome]] (ACS)
 >- [[Pulmonary Edema]] with [[Hypertension]] (to reduce [[Preload]]/[[Afterload]])
 >- [[Hypertensive Emergencies]] with cardiac ischemia

>[!warning]+ Nitro Contraindications #card
> - [[Systolic]] < 100 
> - History of [[Increased Intracranial Pressure]], [[Anemia]], or Allergy
> - Concurrent Exclusionary Medications ([[PDE-5 Inhibitors]])

## Mechanism
**Nitroglycerin** at common **[[therapeutic doses]]** is primarily a [[venodilator]]. This dilation pools blood into the [[venous system]] and reduces [[preload]] to the heart, decreasing cardiac work.

## Administration
**Nitroglycerin** is typically administered [[sublingually]] through tablet or spray at 0.3, 0.4, or 0.6 mg.

>[!protocol] Nitro Administration #card
>Most agencies permit up to **3** doses of **Nitroglycerin** given 5 minutes apart. Any further administration requires permission from [[medical director]]

## Complications
**Nitroglycerin** can have hypotensive effects which can manifest in dizziness, vertigo, weakness, and vomiting. As such, a strict contraindication is low BP
