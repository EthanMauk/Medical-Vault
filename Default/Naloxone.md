# Naloxone  
**Naloxone** is a synthetic opiate blocker used to reverse overdoses.

>[!indicators]+ Indications #card
> - Suspected [[Opioid Overdose]]
> - [[Respiratory Depression]] (Decreased [[Tidal Volume]] or [[Respiratory Rate]] < 8)

>[!warning]+ Contraindications #card
> - No absolute contraindications in prehospital care
> - Allergy or Hypersensitivity to Naloxone

## Administration

>[!protocol]+ Administration #card
>- **[[intramuscular]] (IM)** 2mg via [[auto-injector]] in thigh
>- **[[intranasal]] (IN)** via [[atomizer]] 1mg per nostril

## Mechanism #card
**Naloxone** works as a competitive opioid [[antagonist]] that binds to the μ, κ, and σ-opioid receptors in the [[Central Nervous System]], with the highest affinity for μ. It can reverse respiratory and CNS depression caused by opioid exposure or overdose.

## Considerations  #card
- Does not reverse effects of [[benzodiazepines]]
- Possible violence from patient
- Withdrawal symptoms after administering
