# Epinephrine (medication)
**Epinephrine** is a medication that is used to in life-threatening episodes such as [[Glossary#^anaphylaxis|anaphylaxis]] and [[Glossary#^cardiac-arrest|cardiac arrest]].

>[!indicators|cols]+ Indications #card 
>>- [[Type 1 Allergy Attack]] 
>> - [[Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation]]
>
>> - Severe [[Asthma Attack]]
>>  - [[Septic Shock]]

>[!warning]+ Contraindications #card 
> There are no absolute contraindications in life-threatening episodes.
> Relative cautions: [[Glossary#^hypothermia|hypothermia]], [[Glossary#^hypertension|hypertension]] or [[Glossary#^tachycardia|tachycardia]]
**
## Administration
**Epinephrine** can be administered in multiple ways, but in a **prehospital setting**, the **most common routes** are:
- **[[Intramuscular]] (IM):** for **[[anaphylaxis]]**
- **[[Intravenous]] (IV):** for **[[cardiac arrest]]**

Check that the solution is clear and free of particulates before administration 

>[!field]+ Administration #card
>**IM via auto-injector** at 0.3 mg (0.15 mg for pediatrics) is the most common method for anaphylaxis. Do not inject into hands, feet, or buttocks as it may result in delayed absorption

>[!Protocol]+ LA County Restrictions #card
>Basic providers are not authorized to carry auto-injectors, but may assist patients with their own. Administration of epinephrine requires ALS to be on their way

## Mechanism
Epinephrine works as a [[non-selective agonist]] stimulating α and β adrenergic receptors, causing [[Glossary#^vasoconstriction|vasoconstriction]], [[Glossary#^bronchodilation|bronchodilation]] and increased cardiac activity.

## Considerations
- Epinephrine metabolizes extremely quickly (10 minutes) so immediate transport is required
