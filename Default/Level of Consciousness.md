**Level of Consciousness** is determined by how well the patient responds to external stimuli based on the AVPU scale:

### AVPU Scale
**Awake and Alert:** The patients eyes are open and tracking you, the patient follows commands.

**Responsive to verbal stimuli**: The patient is not alert and awake, but they do open their eyes when you speak to them. The patient may be able to respond in a meaningful way.

**Responsive to painful stimuli**: Patient does not respond to questions, but does respond to painful stimulus. 

**Unresponsive**: Patient does not respond to verbal or physical stimulus. No cough or gag reflex. 

### Orientation
For a patient who is alert or responsive to verbal stimuli, it is important to evaluate their **orientation**, which assesses mental status by determining their awareness of
- **Person** – Who they are
- **Place** – Where they are
- **Time** – Date, day, or approximate time
-  **Event** – What happened / why EMS was called

This is commonly documented as:
**A&O ×4** (Alert and Oriented to person, place, time, and event)

Orientation helps determine brain function, [[Perfusion]], oxygenation, intoxication, head injury.
